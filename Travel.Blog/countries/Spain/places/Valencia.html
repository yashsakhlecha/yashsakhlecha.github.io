Valencia




Valencia- The Spanish city of Oranges.

I visited Valencia on the eve of Las Fallas and our trip was organized by Citylife Madrid. Valencia is a beautiful town and our first sight after entering this city was trees laded with Oranges. Unfortunately, the oranges weren't ripe and could not be eaten.

Las Fallas is a festival were locals spent XX months creating beautiful, colorful statues and fill them up fire crackers. These statues are showcased to visitors for a complete month and then on the eve of Las Fallas, all these statues are simaltaneously lit and it is a spectacle worth witnessing. Apart from all this, there are parades and carnivals throughout the city with young children bursting fire crackers.

Valencia has a beautiful coast and a museum of science, both of which are supposedly know to be very good, however, we just did not end up leaving this part of town for Las Fallas is celebrated in. Valencia also has an active 'Plaza de Torres', where bull fights yet take place.

To summarize, Valencia is a wonderful city and Las Fallas, which is the Spanish equivalent of Diwali is an amazing festival and is definitely worth visitng.
